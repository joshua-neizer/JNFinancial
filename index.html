About Myself

Joseph Neizer - MBA, CPC, CFP, CIM
President

As a Certified Professional Coach (CPC) and a Certified Financial Planner (CFP), I am passionate about financial literacy and helping individuals and families develop financial strategies that enable them to achieve their financial goals and live purpose filled lives.

I have over 20 years coaching experience in the financial services industry as a financial analyst, financial advisor and branch manager.

I have an MBA from the University of Toronto, and I further hold complimentary designation as a Canadian Investment Manager (CIM). I am currently a candidate for the Associate Certified Coach (ACC) designation offered by the International Coaching Federation (IFC).

Outside of work, I volunteer for a number of charitable institutions including churches and other organizations such as Christians Against Poverty (CAP) and as a tax preparer for low-income and new immigrants under CRA’s Community Volunteer Income Tax Program (CVITP).

Throughout my career, I have enjoyed engaging with people from diverse cultural and socio-economic backgrounds. My goal is to continue to provide financial literacy advice on a more personal level. I am a proud husband and father to 4 lovely young adults. In my spare time, I enjoy long walks, playing golf, and spending time with my family.

Mission Statement

To provide individually tailored coaching, education, advice and strategies that encourage healthy personal finances. I have a particular interest in promoting financial literacy to new immigrant and minority communities especially young adults and seniors.

Vision

To help individuals and families anchor their futures on sound financial principles.

A Research Paper By Joseph Neizer
Retirement Coach, CANADA

In order to encourage financial literacy and improve the financial well-being of Canadian families, this research document will define the basic ideas and principles of coaching. The article cites a 2018 Financial Survey of Canadians conducted by the Financial Consumer Agency of Canada (FCAC) to support its conclusions. For Canadians, having adequate finances contributes to a sense of mental and emotional security in daily life. Even while it is not the only factor in people’s anxiety and tension, money plays a big part in exacerbating these problems for both individuals and families. This idea is particularly evident when there is financial and economic uncertainty, as there is at the moment.
